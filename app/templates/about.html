The Scheduler

Welcome to the Mu2e Shift Scheduler! This scheduling tool is designed to assign operations shifts to people throughout the collaboration in a fair and efficient manner. The way we do this is by collecting a list from all collaborators of their prefrences for days and times they are available to take control room shifts. The lists are then fed into an optimization algorithm that finds the best possible assignment of people to shifts based.

The initial schedule is a Monday-Thurday and Friday-Sunday schedule with 3 shifts per day, but the tool is flexible and can be adapted to different shift patterns if needed.

How to get started -- If you are a Mu2e collaborator, go to the "Preferences" page to submit your shift preferences. After submitting your preferences you can check your input and modify your choices.

After everyone has submitted their preferences, the optimizer will be run to generate the schedule. The generated schedule will be available on the results page. This page is also used for dumping out the final schedule and for loading that into the Electronic Logbook (ELOG) system.

About This Tool

The Mu2e Shift Scheduler assigns people to shifts using integer linear programming (ILP) via the PuLP library with the CBC solver.

The optimizer maximises the total preference score — rewarding assignments where a person gets one of their stated preferred shifts — while penalising deviation from a per-person target shift count. Hard per-person minimum and maximum constraints are enforced.

A two-pass approach is used: the first pass solves the full problem; the second pass re-optimises only the shifts that were not filled by a preferred person, using relaxed per-person bounds.
